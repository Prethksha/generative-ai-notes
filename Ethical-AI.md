# Ethical AI

## Key Principles
- Fairness
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Privacy
- Security

## Challenges
- Bias
- Misinformation
- Data Privacy
- Copyright Issues

##Key concepts

1. Generative AI can create original content such as text, images, video, audio, or software code. Although this is exciting, AI can inadvertently cause harm. That’s where ethics as they relate to generative AI become relevant.

2. AI ethics is a multidisciplinary field that studies how to optimize AI’s beneficial impact while reducing risks and adverse outcomes.  

3. Three pillars designed to enhance trust between AI and its users are:
- Transparency, which helps people access information to better understand how an AI tool was created and how it makes decisions
- Accountability, which means organizations take responsibility for the AI tool’s impact
- Fairness, which means the AI tool is designed to minimize bias and promote inclusion

4. To reduce risks related to data inputs in AI tools, organizations should protect data privacy and confidentiality, promote fairness and transparency of data sources, and comply with data usage rights.

5. To promote trustworthy AI-generated content, organizations should prevent harmful and inaccurate content from being generated, manage bias in AI outputs, and respect intellectual property and privacy.

6. The IBM AI risk atlas is an online resource that provides information about some of the risks of working with generative AI, foundation models, and machine learning models.
